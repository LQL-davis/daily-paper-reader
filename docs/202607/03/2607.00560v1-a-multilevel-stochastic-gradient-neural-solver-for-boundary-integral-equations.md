## 摘要
我们开发了一种用于第二类边界积分方程的多级随机梯度神经求解器。未知密度由多层感知机表示，通过在逐级细化的求积网格阶梯上最小化Nyström离散化残差进行训练，每一级使用前一级的参数进行热启动。每一步只需在搭配行的迷你批次上进行稠密矩阵-向量乘积和网络前传，这些操作可直接映射到GPU硬件上。残差收缩由经验神经正切核（NTK）控制，它是连续核的离散采样。在固定网格上，当残差集中在网络收缩缓慢的模式时，训练会停滞，这一平台现象由频率原理描述；谱分析解释并实验证实了，细化求积如何解析更多连续核的谱，并将这些模式重新纳入优化器的可及范围。因此，谱偏倚在其他神经网络求解器中是一种障碍，在此则充当类似多重网格迭代的光滑化器，以求积细化代替粗网格校正。在网络的均匀正则性假设下，总工作量是最细网格上工作量的常数倍，而离散第二类算子的均匀条件数使得NTK成为唯一的速率决定谱，同时将训练残差转换为后验误差界。在内部Dirichlet Laplace/Poisson问题和外部Neumann Helmholtz问题上的实验，使用了参数化和符号距离表面表示，证明了所提出方法与同等容差下的GMRES相比的有效性和效率。

## Abstract
We develop a multilevel stochastic-gradient neural solver for boundary integral equations of the second kind. The unknown density is represented by a multilayer perceptron, trained by minimizing the Nyström-discretized residual on a ladder of refining quadrature grids, each level warm-started from the parameters of the previous one. Each step requires only dense matrix-vector products on mini-batches of collocation rows and network passes, operations that map directly onto GPU hardware. The residual contraction is governed by the empirical neural tangent kernel (NTK), the discrete sample of a single continuum kernel. On a fixed grid, training stalls once the residual concentrates in modes the network contracts slowly, the plateau described by the frequency principle; a spectral analysis explains, and experiments confirm, how refining the quadrature resolves more of the continuum kernel's spectrum and returns these modes to the optimizer's reach. Spectral bias, elsewhere an obstruction to neural network solvers, thus serves as the smoother of a multigrid-type iteration, with quadrature refinement in place of coarse-grid correction. Under a uniform regularity bound on the network, the total work is a constant multiple of the work on the finest grid, and the uniform conditioning of the discrete second-kind operator leaves the NTK as the sole rate-determining spectrum while converting the training residual into an a posteriori error bound. Experiments on interior Dirichlet Laplace/Poisson problems and exterior Neumann Helmholtz problems, using both parametric and signed-distance surface representations, demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method compared with GMRES at comparable tolerances.
