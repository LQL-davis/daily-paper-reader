# Primal-Dual Halpern-PAGE Algorithm for Constrained Stochastic Weakly Convex Optimization *Lixin Tang ‚Ä† Xingyu Wang ‚Ä° and Liwei Zhang ¬ß

Abstract. We tackle the challenging problem of stochastic weakly convex optimization sub-ject to mixed (equality and inequality) expected-value constraints. While optimal O(ŌĶ‚ąí3) sam-ple complexity algorithms exist for unconstrained weakly convex problems, dealing with com-plex functional constraints typically requires cumbersome multi-loop penalty or augmented La-grangian methods, which su ff er from high inner-loop complexity and sensitive parameter tuning. To bridge this fundamental gap, we propose the primal-dual Halpern-PAGE (PD-HP) algorithm. As a purely single-loop method, PD-HP completely bypasses the computational burden of nested iterations. At each step, it merely requires solving a simple strongly convex surrogate subprob-lem alongside a straightforward dual projection, making it exceptionally e ffi cient and convenient to implement. Crucially, we prove that this computationally lightweight algorithm achieves the optimal O(ŌĶ‚ąí3) sample complexity for mixed-constrained stochastic weakly convex problems, successfully matching the theoretical lower bounds. Furthermore, when the primal domain is a compact polyhedral convex set, we establish the deterministic stability of the dual multipliers by exploiting the generalized Mangasarian-Fromovitz constraint qualification (MFCQ) along-side Ho ff man‚Äôs error bound. This ensures that our optimal complexity bound holds strictly under the standard, unbounded KKT residual metric without any theoretical gaps or artificial residual truncations. 

Key words. Stochastic optimization, weakly convex programming, primal-dual method, vari-ance reduction, Halpern iteration, sample complexity, Mangasarian-Fromovitz constraint quali-fication.

# 1 Introduction 

Stochastic weakly convex optimization has recently emerged as a central paradigm in modern com-putational mathematics and machine learning. It o ff ers a unified framework to model problems that are globally nonconvex yet exhibit structured non-smoothness or curvature bounded strictly below by a quadratic function. Such formulations are ubiquitous across diverse applications, including robust statistical learning, phase retrieval, and orthogonal dictionary learning. Despite the widespread success of stochastic algorithms in unconstrained environments, incor-porating complex, data-driven functional constraints (both equalities and inequalities) into weakly convex optimization remains a formidable challenge. Recently, several pivotal works have tackled this constrained setting from various methodological angles. For instance, Jin and Wang [12] estab-lished iteration and sample complexities for a primal-dual algorithm handling inequality constraints. Curtis et al. [4] proved worst-case complexity bounds for an SQP method addressing deterministic nonlinear equalities. Meanwhile, a dominant class of approaches has heavily relied on penalty or augmented Lagrangian methods (ALM). Boob et al. [2] developed a double-loop algorithm for non-convex constrained problems to find an ( ŌĶ, őī )-KKT point. Boob et al. [3] develops stochastic level constrained type methods for solving stochastic problems with composite functions. Similarly, Li et al. [14] designed inexact ALMs for expectation equality constraints, and Shi et al. [22] recently proposed a momentum-based linearized ALM for problems featuring mixed constraints. While these contributions have significantly advanced the field, the existing literature still ex-hibits two major pain points, particularly regarding practical algorithmic e ffi ciency and optimal sample complexity. The first pain point lies in the computational burden of multi-loop architectures. As evidenced by the ALM and penalty-based methods mentioned above [2, 3, 14], functionally con-strained stochastic optimization traditionally demands nested double-loop structures: an outer loop to iteratively update penalty parameters or Lagrangian multipliers, and an inner loop to solve the penalized surrogate subproblems to a prescribed precision. This nested architecture not only intro-duces tremendous computational overhead but also necessitates delicate, ad-hoc tuning of penalty parameters, rendering them highly ine ffi cient for large-scale practical applications. The second pain point is the persistent gap in optimal sample complexity. For unconstrained or simply constrained stochastic weakly convex optimization, the foundational work by Davis and Drusvyatskiy [5] established the Moreau envelope as the standard stationarity metric and proved that standard stochastic methods achieve a sample complexity of O(ŌĶ‚ąí4). Subsequent breakthroughs in variance reduction techniques, such as SPIDER [8] and PAGE [13], successfully pushed this rate to the theoretical lower bound of O(ŌĶ‚ąí3) [1]. However, when transitioning to the mixed-constrained setting, attempting to bypass double-loop structures by deploying standard single-loop stochastic gradient descent ascent (SGDA) algorithms leads to severe trajectory oscillations due to the lack of strong monotonicity. This instability e ff ectively degrades the sample complexity back to a highly suboptimal O(ŌĶ‚ąí4) [16, 19]. A fundamental question naturally arises: Can we design a single-loop, computationally lightweight algorithm for mixed-constrained stochastic weakly convex problems that strictly matches the opti-mal O(ŌĶ‚ąí3) sample complexity? 

In this paper, we answer the above question a ffi rmatively by proposing the Primal-Dual Halpern-PAGE (PD-HP) algorithm. Our algorithmic design is motivated by the elegant integration of two powerful mathematical mechanisms: 

‚ÄĘ Halpern Acceleration: The weak convexity of objectives and constraints yields a KKT oper-2ator that is only weakly monotone. To address this, we draw upon Halpern acceleration for fixed-point and maximal monotone inclusion problems [10, 6, 15] by injecting an anchoring sequence into the primal-dual updates. Acting as a global regularizer, this mechanism anchors the trajectory to prevent divergence, thereby circumventing the heavy computational burden associated with standard proximal point evaluations. 

‚ÄĘ PAGE Variance Reduction: To shatter the O(ŌĶ‚ąí4) complexity barrier, we equip our algorithm with the PAGE mechanism [13]. By intelligently alternating between infrequent large-batch exact gradient evaluations and frequent, computationally cheap small-batch recursive updates, PAGE provides an optimal Markovian variance reduction that is crucial for stabilizing the descent trajectory in high-noise environments. The main contributions of this paper are explicitly summarized as follows: (a) A Novel Purely Single-Loop Algorithm: We develop PD-HP, a fully single-loop primal-dual algorithm for mixed-constrained stochastic weakly convex problems. The subproblems strictly involve a simple strongly convex surrogate minimization for the primal variable and a straightforward thresholding projection for the dual multipliers, completely bypassing the computational burden of nested inner loops and heuristic penalty tuning. (b) Optimal Sample Complexity: We rigorously prove that PD-HP achieves an expected sam-ple complexity of O(ŌĶ‚ąí3) to find an ŌĶ-approximate KKT point. This optimally matches the theoretical lower bound of the unconstrained setting, thereby completely closing the sample complexity gap for mixed-constrained weakly convex stochastic optimization. (c) Exact Geometric Equivalence and Unbounded KKT Guarantees: A prevalent limitation in existing literature is the reliance on artificially truncated residuals to evaluate convergence. We overcome this by rigorously exploiting the generalized Mangasarian-Fromovitz constraint qualification (MFCQ) alongside Ho ff man‚Äôs Lemma for polyhedral domains. By establishing an exact geometric equivalence‚Äďwith a condition number of precisely őļ = 1‚Äďbetween the Moreau envelope gradient and the restricted KKT mapping, and by proving the deterministic stability of the dual multipliers, we strictly validate our optimal O(ŌĶ‚ąí3) complexity under the standard, physically meaningful unbounded KKT residual metric with high probability. 

Organization of the paper. The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section 2 for-mally defines the mixed-constrained problem, establishes the uniform multiplier bounds via the Generalized MFCQ, and presents the proposed PD-HP algorithm. Section 3 provides the rigorous complexity analysis, establishing the optimal O(ŌĶ‚ąí3) sample complexity under both restricted and unbounded KKT metrics. Section 4 presents numerical experiments that empirically validate our theoretical claims. Finally, Section 5 concludes the paper and discusses future research directions.
