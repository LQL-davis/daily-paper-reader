Abstract 

In this paper, we devise a prox -based semi-smooth Newton method that is applicable to a finite element discretization of a broad class of nonsmooth convex variational problems, including the TV-minimization problem, the p-Dirichlet problem, the obstacle problem, and the elasto-plastic torsion problem. To this end, on the basis of the proximity operator, the discrete primal-dual optimality conditions are reformulated as nonlinear operator equations with Newton-differentiable structure. Under suitable assumptions on the energy densities, we establish the global well-posedness and local super-linear convergence of the resulting semi-smooth Newton method. The proposed approach coincides with established semi-smooth Newton methods for obstacle-type problems, satisfies a primal-dual invariance, and, under suitable additional assumptions, is globally well-posed in the infinite-dimensional setting .

Keywords: Proximity operator; semi-smooth Newton method; convex variational problems; Fenchel duality; finite element method; total variation minimization; p-Dirichlet problem; elasto-plastic torsion

6.2 p-Dirichlet problem 

In this subsection, we consider the p-Dirichlet problem (cf . [ 32 , 34 ]), a nonlinear generalization of the classical Dirichlet problem arising in the modelling of non-Newtonian fluid flow and nonlin-ear elasticity. Originating from the early studies of power-law rheology ( cf . In contrast to the linear case p = 2, however, the design of robust nonlinear solvers for the p-Dirichlet problem is delicate, and there appears to be no universally preferred solution strategy across the full range p вҲҲ (1 , +вҲһ).

7. Numerical experiments 

In this section, we carry out numerical experiments reviewing the findings on the prox -based semi-smooth Newton methods derived in Section 6, i.e. , for the TV-minimization problem, the

7.1 Implementation details 

All experiments were conducted using the finite element software package FEniCS (version 2019.1.0, cf . [ 35 ]). All graphics were generated using the Matplotlib library (version 3.5.1, cf . [ 27 ]). 

7.1.1 Linear solver 

The linear systems (5.6) arising in the semi-smooth Newton iteration in Algorithm 5.1 are solved according to the procedure outlined in Remark 5.14. In particular, the symmetric positive definite linear systems (5.26) for the computation of the primal update direction are solved by a sparse Cholesky factorization using CHOLMOD [15 ] through the Python package scikit-sparse 

(version 0.5.0, cf . [ 46 ]). The same direct solver is used for the linear systems arising in the primal semi-smooth Newton method. 

7.1.2 Computation of proximity operators and Newton derivatives 

For the TV-minimization problem ( cf . Subsection 6.1) and the elasto-plastic torsion problem (cf . Subsection 6.3), the proximity operator and its Newton derivative are computed using the ex-plicit representation formulas (6.9) and (6.29) , respectively. In the case of the p-Dirichlet problem (cf . Subsection 6.2), the proximity operator is computed according to the procedure outlined in Re-mark 6.2, while its Newton derivative is evaluated via the implicit representation formula (6.19a) .
