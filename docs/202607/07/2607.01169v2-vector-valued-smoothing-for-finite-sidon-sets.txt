VECTOR-VALUED SMOOTHING FOR FINITE SIDON SETS 

JIANFENG HOU AND HONGBIN ZHAO 

Abstract. Let F (N ) denote the largest cardinality of a Sidon subset of {0, 1, . . . , N − 1}. We prove 

F (N ) ≤ N 1/2 + 0 .9435 N 1/4 + O(1) .

The argument uses a vector-valued convolution inequality in which several smoothing kernels cooperate to produce a boundary majorant while their 

L2 energies are averaged. For fixed kernels and mixing weights, the boundary optimization is a strictly convex quadratic program with an explicit dual. An eight-kernel numerical candidate is converted into a finite rational certificate and verified using exact arithmetic only. 

1. Introduction 

A finite set A ⊂ Z is Sidon if every nonzero difference has at most one ordered representation; equivalently, 

a − b = c − d̸ = 0 , a, b, c, d ∈ A, 

implies (a, b ) = ( c, d ). Write 

F (N ) := max {|A| : A ⊂ { 0, 1, . . . , N − 1} is Sidon }.

The classical construction of Singer [ 7] gives F (N ) ≥ N 1/2 +O(1) for infinitely many N . Erdős and Turán [4] proved 

F (N ) ≤ N 1/2 + N 1/4 + O(1) ,

and Lindström [ 5 ] later obtained the same secondary term by a different argument. The coefficient of N 1/4 was subsequently reduced to 0.998 , 0.99703 ,and 0.98183 [1, 6 , 2 ]. More recently, Tao reported joint work with Carter, Georgiev, Gómez-Serrano, Hunter, O’Bryant and Wagner on a computer-assisted refinement of the Carter–Hunter–O’Bryant argument, yielding the coefficient 0.97633 [ 3 , 8 ]. We give a fully explicit rational certificate below 

0.9435 .

Theorem 1.1. There is an explicit constant γ0 = 0 .943492590 . . . < 0.9435 

such that 

F (N ) ≤ N 1/2 + γ0N 1/4 + O(1) .

5. Concluding remarks 

The certificate was found numerically, but the optimization procedure is not part of the proof. Once the rational vectors are fixed, the argument reduces to the general analytic lemma and a finite list of exact inequalities. The multi-kernel formulation is more flexible than averaging scalar bounds: no individual kernel is required to satisfy a boundary covering inequality on its own; only their weighted combination is constrained. It would be interesting to optimize the finite problem systematically or to allow more kernels. A different direction is to introduce controlled cross-kernel terms whose combined correlation kernel remains nonnegative at every nonzero shift. Positive semidefiniteness of the coefficient matrix alone is not sufficient for the Sidon difference estimate, so any such extension would require additional correlation-positivity constraints.
